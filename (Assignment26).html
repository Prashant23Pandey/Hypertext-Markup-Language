Understanding CSS Margin and Padding

This is a DIV element.

It has 30px *padding* (inside space, light blue area extends here) and 40px *margin* (outside space, transparent area pushes it away).

Key Concepts:

  • *Padding* creates space *inside* an element, between the content and its border. The background color extends into the padding area.
  • *Margin* creates space *outside* the element's border, separating it from adjacent elements. This area is transparent.
  • The `margin: 40px;` shorthand sets all four sides (top, right, bottom, left) to 40 pixels.
  • The `padding: 30px;` shorthand sets all four sides of the padding to 30 pixels.
